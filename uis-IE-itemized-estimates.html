UIS-IE: Itemized Estimates

Customer-facing line-item estimates with quantities, unit costs, per-line markup, and one-click conversion to Simple invoices. Complements the existing GL-based estimate worksheet (EstimateChangeOrderRecordValue + AccountItem) by adding a discrete EstimateLineItem model for catalog-driven and free-form rows. Unblocks residential and commercial quoting workflows (Clement Construction, Tight 5, Vanos) and aligns with T&M Work Order line-item patterns.

1. Business Case

Today’s estimates in Appello are built as a budgetary COGS worksheet keyed by chart-of-accounts AccountItem rows. That model supports internal job costing and profitability tracking but does not match how many customers quote: discrete priced lines (labor hours × rate, materials × unit, equipment rental) with per-line markup and a client-ready PDF.

Customer Evidence

“What everybody actually wants is that itemized estimate. Clement Construction is screaming about it because the way they work is residential quoting.” — Internal product discussion (ATLAS transcript, April 2026)
“They need to understand if the job’s $120,000, what are they getting done for $120,000? … a broken-down estimate, we get that in QuickBooks. You get that with BuilderTrend.” — Clement Construction onboarding (ATLAS)

3. Architecture Overview

Key decision: Add EstimateLineItem as a parallel line store to the existing EstimateChangeOrderRecordValue worksheet. Itemized estimates use creationType = Itemized (new enum value on EstimateChangeOrderRecord) and primarily drive UI, PDF, and invoice conversion from EstimateLineItem[]. The legacy account-item grid remains for budgetary / Full worksheet mode.

Data Flow

Estimator → Estimate (type = Estimate, creationType = Itemized)
    + EstimateLineItem[] (labor/material/equipment/custom)
    → Auto unit cost from TradeLevelRate / MaterialPricing / EquipmentRateValue
    → Per-line markup → price

Office → Generate PDF (IE-4) → customer
Office → Won → createInvoiceFromEstimate (IE-5) → JobBill (Simple) + JobBillLineItem[]

Prerequisites

LEM-1 and LEM-2 should be available for labor and equipment unit-cost resolution on structured rows. LEM-3 improves material sell-price resolution; IE-1 can stub material pricing until LEM-3 ships.
