1 · Business Case & Customer Evidence

▼
"Four years ago, my friend Matt Vanos said, I have a material management problem. Come solve it." — Corey Shelson, on the Vanos Insulation partnership

Value Proposition

💰
Multi-Supplier Pricing
Same product, different prices from different distributors. Best-price visibility at the point of ordering.
🏭
Manufacturer Normalization
Track manufacturer SKUs, UPCs, and MSRPs independent of distributor pricing for a single source of truth.
👥
Company Integration
Manufacturer and Supplier are Company types — reuse existing Company CRUD, contacts, notes, and files.
🛡
Foundation for Procurement
Every downstream feature (Price Book Import, Supplier POs, Discounts, Receiving) depends on this data model.

Competitive Landscape

HAS Procore Supplier management + PO creation with multi-vendor pricing
HAS ServiceTitan Product catalog with integrated supplier pricing and purchase workflow
HAS BuildOps Material management with supplier relationships and pricing
PARTIAL Appello (today) Material catalog exists but no product-supplier pricing relationship

2 · Scope

▼

IN SCOPE

  • 2 new Prisma models: ManufacturerProduct, DistributorPrice
  • “Manufacturer” company type seeded via PropertyValue + CompanyPayableType
  • Relations on Company (ManufacturerProducts, DistributorPrices), Material (ManufacturerProducts)
  • Optional distributorId FK on MaterialOrder
  • Products tab on Manufacturer company detail page
  • Products & Pricing tab on Supplier company detail page
  • Supply Chain section on Material detail page (Manufacturers + Distributor Pricing sub-tables)
  • “Best price” indicator across distributors for same manufacturer product
  • GraphQL CRUD for ManufacturerProduct and DistributorPrice

OUT OF SCOPE

  • Bulk price book import — deferred to MAT-3
  • Discount management — deferred to MAT-9
  • Supplier PO creation from pricing data — deferred to MAT-5
  • Mobile material browsing by supplier
  • Inventory integration

4 · Data Model

▼

ManufacturerProduct

model ManufacturerProduct {
    id                String   @id @default(cuid()) @db.VarChar(21)
    materialId        String   @db.VarChar(21)
    Material          Material @relation(fields: [materialId], references: [id])
    manufacturerId    String   @db.VarChar(21)
    Manufacturer      Company  @relation("ManufacturerProducts", fields: [manufacturerId], references: [id])
    manufacturerSku   String   @db.VarChar(100)
    msrp              Decimal? @db.Decimal(10, 2)
    upc               String?  @db.VarChar(50)
    description       String?  @db.Text
    packageQty        Decimal? @db.Decimal(10, 2)
    packageUnit       String?  @db.VarChar(50)
    isActive          Boolean  @default(true)
    isDeleted         Boolean  @default(false)
    createdAt         DateTime @default(now())
    updatedAt         DateTime @updatedAt

    DistributorPrices DistributorPrice[]

    @@unique([materialId, manufacturerId, manufacturerSku])
    @@index([materialId])
    @@index([manufacturerId])
    @@map("ManufacturerProduct")
}
FieldTypePurpose
materialIdFK → MaterialLinks to the generic material in the catalog
manufacturerIdFK → CompanyThe manufacturer company (type = “Manufacturer”)
manufacturerSkuVarChar(100)Manufacturer’s own part number / SKU
msrpDecimal(10,2)Manufacturer’s suggested retail price
upcVarChar(50)Universal product code / barcode
packageQtyDecimal(10,2)Quantity per package (e.g. 126 ft)
packageUnitVarChar(50)Unit of measure for packaging (e.g. ft/box)
isActiveBooleanSoft active flag for product lifecycle

DistributorPrice

model DistributorPrice {
    id                      String              @id @default(cuid()) @db.VarChar(21)
    manufacturerProductId   String              @db.VarChar(21)
    ManufacturerProduct     ManufacturerProduct @relation(fields: [manufacturerProductId], references: [id])
    distributorId           String              @db.VarChar(21)
    Distributor             Company             @relation("DistributorPrices", fields: [distributorId], references: [id])
    distributorSku          String              @db.VarChar(100)
    unitPrice               Decimal             @db.Decimal(10, 2)
    listPrice               Decimal?            @db.Decimal(10, 2)
    discountPercent         Decimal?            @db.Decimal(5, 2)
    effectiveFrom           DateTime?
    effectiveTo             DateTime?
    minQty                  Int?
    leadTimeDays            Int?
    isPreferred             Boolean             @default(false)
    isInStock               Boolean?
    notes                   String?             @db.Text
    isDeleted               Boolean             @default(false)
    createdAt               DateTime            @default(now())
    updatedAt               DateTime            @updatedAt

    @@unique([manufacturerProductId, distributorId, distributorSku])
    @@index([manufacturerProductId])
    @@index([distributorId])
    @@map("DistributorPrice")
}
FieldTypePurpose
manufacturerProductIdFK → ManufacturerProductThe specific manufacturer product being priced
distributorIdFK → CompanyThe distributor / supplier company
distributorSkuVarChar(100)Distributor’s own SKU for this product
unitPriceDecimal(10,2)Actual price charged by distributor
listPriceDecimal(10,2)Published list price before discounts
discountPercentDecimal(5,2)Discount off list price (computed or negotiated)
effectiveFrom / ToDateTimeDate range for price validity
minQtyIntMinimum order quantity for this price
leadTimeDaysIntExpected delivery lead time in days
isPreferredBooleanMarks this as the preferred distributor for this product
isInStockBooleanCurrent stock availability (optional)

Seed SQL — Manufacturer Company Type

-- Add "Manufacturer" to PropertyValue for companyType
INSERT INTO PropertyValue (id, instanceId, propertyId, value, sortOrder, isArchived, isDeleted, createdAt, updatedAt)
SELECT
    'mfr_type_seed_001',
    pv.instanceId,
    pv.propertyId,
    'Manufacturer',
    (SELECT COALESCE(MAX(sortOrder), 0) + 1 FROM PropertyValue WHERE propertyId = pv.propertyId AND instanceId = pv.instanceId),
    false,
    false,
    NOW(),
    NOW()
FROM PropertyValue pv
WHERE pv.value = 'Supplier'
  AND pv.propertyId IN (SELECT id FROM Property WHERE name = 'companyType')
  AND NOT EXISTS (
    SELECT 1 FROM PropertyValue pv2
    WHERE pv2.propertyId = pv.propertyId
      AND pv2.instanceId = pv.instanceId
      AND pv2.value = 'Manufacturer'
  )
GROUP BY pv.instanceId, pv.propertyId;

-- Add CompanyPayableType for Manufacturer (AP=false, AR=false)
INSERT INTO CompanyPayableType (id, companyType, isAccountsPayable, isAccountsReceivable, createdAt, updatedAt)
SELECT
    'mfr_payable_seed',
    'Manufacturer',
    false,
    false,
    NOW(),
    NOW()
FROM DUAL
WHERE NOT EXISTS (
    SELECT 1 FROM CompanyPayableType WHERE companyType = 'Manufacturer'
);

Consumed Queries (Existing)

  • companyDetail — Used for Manufacturer/Supplier type check and header rendering
  • materials / materialDetail — Used for linking products to materials and rendering material context

Computed Field: effectivePrice

The effectivePrice field on DistributorPrice is a GraphQL resolver-computed value. If discountPercent is set, it returns listPrice × (1 − discountPercent / 100); otherwise it returns unitPrice. This enables consistent “best price” comparison across distributors.

7 · Acceptance Criteria

▼
  • ManufacturerProduct and DistributorPrice tables created with unique constraints and indexes
  • “Manufacturer” company type seeded in PropertyValue with CompanyPayableType record (AP=false, AR=false)
  • Company creation form shows “Manufacturer” as a type option
  • Manufacturer company detail shows “Products” tab with ManufacturerProduct table
  • Supplier company detail shows “Products & Pricing” tab with DistributorPrice table
  • Material detail page shows “Supply Chain” section with Manufacturers + Distributor Pricing sub-tables
  • Quick-add: link manufacturer product to material, link distributor pricing to manufacturer product
  • “Best price” indicator highlights lowest unitPrice across distributors for same manufacturer product
  • MaterialOrder can optionally reference a distributor Company
  • GraphQL queries: manufacturerProducts(materialId), distributorPrices(distributorId) return correct data
  • Relations resolve correctly: Material ↔ ManufacturerProduct ↔ DistributorPrice, Company as Manufacturer/Distributor
  • No regression on existing Material catalog, Company pages, or MaterialOrder functionality
  • Permission: users need material.view to see Supply Chain section, material.edit to link products
  • Validation: manufacturerSku required; materialId + manufacturerId + manufacturerSku unique constraint enforced with user-friendly error
