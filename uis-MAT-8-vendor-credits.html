1 · Business Case & Customer Evidence ▼

Returns happen on every job. Without credit tracking, job costs are overstated and AP balances are wrong. Credits are different from refunds — a credit means the supplier is holding a balance for future use.

Customer Evidence

"Credits are different from refunds. A credit means they’re saying, hey, you have $3,000 on your account now that you can buy stuff from us for in the future." — Internal discussion (Oct 2025)
"If a vendor has a credit, why would they give credits? Common scenarios: returns, overpayments, or partial shipments." — Travis (Oct 2025)

Value Delivered

🔗
Credit-to-PO Linkage
Credits are linked back to the originating purchase order and its line items for full traceability.
📈
Job Cost Adjustment
Approved credits reduce the material cost on the linked job in the Financial Summary.
📦
Inventory Return Movement
Physical returns create outbound inventory movements (MAT-7) so stock levels stay accurate.
💰
AP Reconciliation
Credit memos provide the data needed to reconcile accounts payable with suppliers.

4 · Data Model ▼

VendorCredit

model VendorCredit {
  id              String    @id @db.VarChar(21)
  isDeleted       Boolean   @default(false)
  isArchived      Boolean   @default(false)
  createdAt       DateTime  @default(now())
  updatedAt       DateTime  @default(now()) @updatedAt
  creditNumber    String    @db.VarChar(100)
  supplierId      String    @db.VarChar(21)
  Supplier        Company   @relation("VendorCredits", fields: [supplierId], references: [id])
  purchaseOrderId String?   @db.VarChar(21)
  PurchaseOrder   PurchaseOrder? @relation(fields: [purchaseOrderId], references: [id])
  jobId           String?   @db.VarChar(21)
  Job             Job?      @relation(fields: [jobId], references: [id])
  reasonId        String?   @db.VarChar(21)
  status          String    @default("Draft") @db.VarChar(50)  // PropertyValue-based (user-configurable, Kanban-ready)
  amount          Decimal?  @db.Decimal(12, 2)
  notes           String?   @db.Text
  LineItems       VendorCreditLineItem[]
  @@index([supplierId, isDeleted])
  @@index([purchaseOrderId, isDeleted])
}

VendorCreditLineItem

model VendorCreditLineItem {
  id                      String    @id @db.VarChar(21)
  isDeleted               Boolean   @default(false)
  createdAt               DateTime  @default(now())
  updatedAt               DateTime  @default(now()) @updatedAt
  vendorCreditId          String    @db.VarChar(21)
  VendorCredit            VendorCredit @relation(fields: [vendorCreditId], references: [id])
  purchaseOrderLineItemId String?   @db.VarChar(21)
  description             String?   @db.VarChar(500)
  quantity                Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 4)
  unitPrice               Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  @@index([vendorCreditId, isDeleted])
}

Total on VendorCreditLineItem is computed (quantity × unitPrice). VendorCredit.amount is the sum of all line item totals.

Status Constants

StatusDescription
DraftCredit created but not yet submitted to supplier
SubmittedCredit submitted and awaiting supplier confirmation
ApprovedSupplier confirmed the credit; job cost adjustment applied
AppliedCredit has been applied against future purchases or reconciled in AP

Reason Codes (PropertyValue Seeds)

ReasonUse Case
ReturnPhysical return of materials to supplier
DamagedMaterials arrived damaged; supplier issues credit
OverpaymentInvoice was overpaid; supplier issues credit for difference
Short ShipmentFewer items received than invoiced
Price AdjustmentSupplier adjusts price after invoicing (e.g., volume discount)

7 · Acceptance Criteria ▼

  • A vendor credit can be created from a Supplier PO detail page with pre-filled supplier and PO reference
  • Credit line items can be linked to specific PO line items with quantity and unit price
  • When reason is Return, approving the credit creates an outbound InventoryMovement of type Return (MAT-7)
  • Approved credits reduce material costs on the linked job in the Financial Summary (JobFinancialSummaryService)
  • Credit memo PDF can be generated and downloaded from the credit detail
  • Reason codes are loaded from PropertyValue seeds: Return, Damaged, Overpayment, Short Shipment, Price Adjustment
  • Credit numbers follow the VC-#### auto-increment pattern
  • Credits follow the status workflow: Draft → Submitted → Approved → Applied
  • The applyCredit mutation transitions status to Applied and triggers all downstream effects (inventory movement, cost adjustment)
  • Permission checks enforced: vendorCredit.view, vendorCredit.create, vendorCredit.edit, vendorCredit.delete
  • Validation: quantity must be greater than 0, supplier is required, credit cannot be approved without at least one line item
  • PO detail page shows a Credits tab listing all credits against that PO with net total calculation
