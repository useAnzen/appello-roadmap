1 · Business Case & Customer Evidence ▼

Structured line items are what separates a first-class work order system from a form-based workaround. Without discrete labor, material, and equipment rows, there is no way to auto-price, subtotal, or generate accurate invoices. Auto-pricing from existing rate tables (TradeLevel, Material catalog, Equipment rates) means field workers don't need to know rates — they select a category and the system fills in the rest.

Customer Evidence

"Selecting labor categories should auto-populate rates, markups, and taxes. Our field guys shouldn't have to look up rates — they just pick 'Journeyman' and enter hours." — Tear Systems (Product Discovery)
"We need separate sections for labor, materials, and equipment on every T&M ticket. Each has different markup rules." — AB Insulation (Requirements Workshop)
⚡
Auto-Pricing
Rates pulled from TradeLevel, Material, and Equipment tables — no manual entry of dollar amounts by field workers.
📊
Structured Data
Discrete rows enable accurate subtotals, markup calculations, and downstream invoice generation.
🔗
Rate Table Integration
Leverages existing TradeLevel and Material models — no new rate management UI needed.
📱
Mobile-Ready Data
Repeating-row structure works identically on desktop and mobile (WO-8).

2 · Scope ▼

In Scope

  • IN 3 new Prisma models: WorkOrderLaborItem, WorkOrderMaterialItem, WorkOrderEquipmentItem
  • IN Auto-pricing service that pulls rates from TradeLevel, Material, and Equipment tables
  • IN Repeating-row editor components for each line item type
  • IN Row-level total calculation (hours × rate for each pay type)
  • IN Upsert mutation for batch save of all line items
  • IN Recalculate totals mutation to update Job subtotals and grandTotal

Out of Scope

  • OUT Drag-and-drop reordering (Phase 1 uses manual orderNo)
  • OUT Bulk import from external price sheets
  • OUT Custom column visibility per user

model WorkOrderLaborItem {
  id                String    @id @default(nanoid()) @db.VarChar(21)
  instanceId        String    @db.VarChar(21)
  jobId             String    @db.VarChar(21)
  tradeLevelId      String?   @db.VarChar(21)
  description       String?   @db.VarChar(500)
  straightTimeHours Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  overtimeHours     Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  doubleTimeHours   Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  rate              Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  overtimeRate      Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  doubleTimeRate    Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  total             Decimal?  @db.Decimal(10, 2)
  orderNo           Int       @default(0)
  isDeleted         Boolean   @default(false)
  createdAt         DateTime  @default(now())
  updatedAt         DateTime  @updatedAt

  Instance          Instance  @relation(fields: [instanceId], references: [id])

7 · Acceptance Criteria ▼

  • Labor items can be created with a TradeLevel selection and the rate, OT rate (1.5×), and DT rate (2×) are auto-populated
  • Material items can be created with a Material selection and the unit cost is auto-populated from the Material catalog
  • Equipment items can be created with an Equipment selection and the unit cost is auto-populated
  • Row-level totals calculate correctly: Labor = (ST × Rate) + (OT × OT Rate) + (DT × DT Rate); Material/Equipment = Quantity × Unit Cost
  • The upsertWorkOrderLineItems mutation saves all 3 item types in a single transaction
  • The recalculateWorkOrderTotals mutation updates the Job's laborSubtotal, materialSubtotal, equipmentSubtotal, and grandTotal
  • Deleting a line item (setting isDeleted: true) recalculates totals and removes the row from the UI
  • The repeating-row editor allows adding, editing, and removing rows without a page reload
